# Abstract

Soil water retention capacity and soil structure are fundamental properties that influence agroecosystems, for instance plant growth, nutrient cycling, and hydromechanical dynamics. The soil water retention capacity (SWRC) determines the availability of moisture for sustaining crops, and collectively regulates infiltration, storage, and drainage. Similarly, soil structure controls aeration, root penetration, and microbial activity, thereby shaping the physical environment for plant development. Understanding these parameters and how they change in agroecosystem is critical for modelling water flow, predicting crop responses under variable climatic conditions, and designing sustainable management practices. This is because poor soil structure can lead to compaction, erosion, and reduced hydraulic conductivity and thus water movement, impairing productivity and crop yields. Integrating geophysical techniques for monitoring soil condition enables non-invasive characterization of these properties at field scale, supporting precision agriculture and soil conservation strategies.
